# Quality Checklist

Use this checklist before delivering a technical blog article.

## Core coverage

- Does the article cover the core knowledge points for the requested scope?
- Are important prerequisite concepts introduced before being used?
- Are key terms connected to each other instead of listed separately?
- Does the article avoid obvious gaps that would confuse a beginner?

## Structure

- Is the article structure clear?
- Does the structure fit the task rather than follow a rigid template?
- Does the section order create a reasonable learning path?
- Are optional sections included only when useful?

## Reader fit

- Is the article suitable for the user and beginner learners?
- Does it build intuition before diving into details?
- Are hard concepts explained with examples, diagrams, or step-by-step reasoning?
- Would the user be able to review the article later and quickly recover the main ideas?

## Principle clarity

- Does the article actually explain the principle or mechanism?
- Does it answer “why” and “how,” not just “what”?
- Are boundary conditions, tradeoffs, or common misunderstandings included when important?

## Diagrams/images

- Does each image materially improve understanding?
- Is each image explained in nearby text?
- Are image names and alt text meaningful?
- Are uploaded URLs stable and inserted into the Markdown?
- Are there no broken local image placeholders unless the user requested a local draft?

## Research and correctness

- Were user-provided materials prioritized?
- Were official or authoritative sources used when needed?
- Are version-specific claims verified or qualified?
- Are external references included when they materially informed the article?
- Are uncertain claims avoided or clearly qualified?

## Markdown publishing quality

- Is the output valid Markdown?
- Are headings nested clearly?
- Are code blocks labeled when useful?
- Are tables readable?
- Are there no leftover TODOs or placeholders?
- Does the article have a useful final summary?

## Final summary quality

The final summary should help the user review the topic later. It should capture:

- What the topic is
- Why it matters
- The core mechanism or conclusion
- The most important things to remember
